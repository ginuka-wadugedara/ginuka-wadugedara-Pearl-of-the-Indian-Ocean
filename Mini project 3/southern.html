Southern province

The Southern Province of Sri Lanka is a small geographic area consisting of three districts: Galle and Hambantota. Farming and fishing are the main sources of income for the vast majority of the people in this region. Important landmarks of the Southern Province include the wildlife sanctuaries; Yala, Lunugamvehera and Bundala National Parks. Districts under Southern Province

Galle District

The 17th c.Dutch fort at galle, close to the island's southernmost point, 173 km from colombo, has the distinction of being the best-preserved sea fort in south Asia. Enter through the imposing stone bastions that encricle the sea-facing promontory and step back in time. inside the Fort you will find that it exudes old-world charm.The narrow streets are dotted with Dutch colonial villas and there's welcome adsence of vehicular traffic. There are several museums and antique shops that display curiosities from the island's colonial era. of the many colonial buildingd, perhaps the most absorbing is the Dutch Reformed Church, containing ornately carved memorials to the city's Dutch settler.The Fort also hosts some of the island's most exclusive boutique-style accommodation in former villas restored to their colonial glory.

Hambantota District

Elephant may be the celebrated of Sri lanka's diverse wildlife, but they actully represent just one actor on a crowded ecological stage. For instance, this is one of the best places in the world to see the elusive leopard, and one of the only places where the big cat is top predator in the system-the king of the Sri lanka jungle. With string markings, a sleek, powerful body,and graceful movement, the leopard is thrilling to observe-try Yala National Park in the south-east coastal arid zone.
